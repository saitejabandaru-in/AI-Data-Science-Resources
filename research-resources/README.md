## 2. How to Read a Research Paper (The Three-Pass Method)

Attempting to read a paper start-to-finish in detail can lead to burnout. The **Three-Pass Method** (S. Keshav) provides a structured alternative.

### Pass 1: The Quick Scan (5-10 mins)
Determine if the paper is worth reading further.
1.  **Read the Title, Abstract, and Introduction.**
2.  **Read section and sub-section headings.**
3.  **Inspect the mathematical equations/formulas and look at diagrams/plots.**
4.  **Read the Conclusion.**
5.  **Scan the references** to see what papers are cited.

### Pass 2: Grasping the Logic (1 hour)
Understand the paper's core line of reasoning.
*   Read the paper with care, but ignore details like complex proofs.
*   Focus on key diagrams, graphs, and illustrations. Pay attention to axes, legends, and error bars.
*   **Write down key questions** or concepts you do not understand.

### Pass 3: Detailed Reconstruction (3-4 hours)
Fully understand the paper by virtually re-implementing it.
*   Attempt to mentally reconstruct the paper: make the same assumptions and derive the same equations.
*   Compare your reconstruction against the actual paper. Note where you succeed and fail.
*   Identify all underlying assumptions, failure modes, and unexplored engineering trade-offs.

## 3. Search Engines & Research Tools

### Academic Repositories
*   **[arXiv](https://arxiv.org/)**: The premier repository for open-access preprints in computer science, physics, and mathematics.
*   **[Semantic Scholar](https://www.semanticscholar.org/)**: AI-backed academic search engine that extracts citations, key topics, and paper connections.
*   **[OpenAlex](https://openalex.org/)**: A massive open database of scholarly documents, authors, and citations.

### Literature Discovery Tools
*   **[Connected Papers](https://www.connectedpapers.com/)**: Generates visual graphs of related academic papers based on co-citation analysis.
*   **[ResearchRabbit](https://www.researchrabbit.ai/)**: A tool that helps you map citation networks, find related work, and organize bibliography collections.
